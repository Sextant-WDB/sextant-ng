Demo of one naive approach to showing user-specific data

Here’s one way we could approach this: when you `npm install` the package, you get a totally generic tracking script/plugin that, just like everyone else’s, posts data to our server. When you create an account on our dashboard, though, you specify the URL (eventually URLs) you’d like to track, and the server only exposes data that corresponds to that site.

It mostly works, as you can see: try logging in with email `1@1.com` / password `1`, email `2@2.com` / password `2`, or email `3@3.com` / password `3`. (Free free to add sample data if you want.)

On the other hand:

  • What about localhost:xxxx, which is pretty much the same for everyone?
  • What’s to stop me from registering with your URL when I create my account?
